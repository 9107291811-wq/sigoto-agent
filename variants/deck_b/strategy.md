# Deck B Strategy

Deck B is the Okidogi baseline with a Neutralization Zone package.

Deck change from Deck A:

- Remove 1 Legacy Energy.
- Remove 2 Battle Cage.
- Add 1 Neutralization Zone.
- Add 2 Colress's Tenacity.

Neutralization Zone rules:

- If the opponent has publicly shown an ex Pokemon in play, in discard, or through logs, play Neutralization Zone during our turn.
- If no opponent ex Pokemon is visible yet, hold Neutralization Zone instead of playing it blindly.
- The deck is mostly safe under Neutralization Zone because Okidogi, Solrock, Lunatone, Binacle, and Barbaracle are not ex Pokemon.
- Lillie's Clefairy ex is affected differently because it is itself an ex Pokemon. If both Pokemon are ex, damage can still happen.
- Dragapult ex can still place damage counters through effects, so keep the Clefairy plan against Dragapult ex.
- Against non-Dragapult ex decks, do not bring out Clefairy later just because Neutralization Zone is active.

Colress's Tenacity rules:

- Colress's Tenacity searches any Energy and a Stadium, so it can find Prism Energy plus Neutralization Zone.
- If Energy is missing and the opponent has a visible ex Pokemon, Colress's Tenacity is the highest-priority Supporter.
- If Energy is missing even without visible ex Pokemon, Colress's Tenacity is still a strong Supporter because the Energy search is guaranteed.
- If Neutralization Zone is prized, Colress's Tenacity can still take Energy, so it remains usable.
- When using deck-search cards such as Fighting Gong, Poké Pad, or Hilda, remember whether Neutralization Zone appears to be in deck or likely prized.
